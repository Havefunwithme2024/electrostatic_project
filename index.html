Pollution and contamination

The presence of industrial pollutants, chemicals, heavy metals, microplastics, and emerging contaminants like pharmaceuticals makes water treatment complex. These contaminants are hard to remove and often require advanced technologies.

Resource and Energy Intensity

Many water treatment processes are energy-intensive and costly. This is especially problematic in regions with limited resources, as the high energy and operational costs can be prohibitive.

Physical Treatment Techniques

Sedimentation and Filtration: Removing larger particles and sediments through gravity settling tanks and fine filtration. Sand Filtration: Effective for small particles and microbes; layers of sand filter out contaminants.

Aeration: Adding oxygen to water, which helps remove gases like carbon dioxide and volatile organic compounds, and can reduce odor and improve water taste.

Chemical Treatment Methods and PowerPure

Chlorination: Disinfection through chlorination kills harmful bacteria and pathogens. Ozonation: Using ozone gas, which is a powerful oxidant, to disinfect and oxidize organic and inorganic materials. Advanced Oxidation Processes (AOPs): Combining ozone, hydrogen peroxide, or UV light to break down complex contaminants like pharmaceuticals. PowerPure is an electrical-powered water filtration system that uses electricity and water to filter out impurities. Using a technique called ultrafiltration, the electrical-powered system squeezes water through special membranes that contain tiny 20-nanometer holes.

Sand and Gravel

Used in sand filters for sediment and pathogen removal.

Activated Carbon

Adsorbs organic compounds, chlorine, and certain heavy metals. Commonly used in point-of-use water filters.

Aluminium Sulfate

: A widely used coagulant that helps particles clump together, making them easier to filter out.

Ferric Chloride and Ferric Sulfate

Iron-based coagulants, also effective for removing fine particles.

Electrolytic cell

In the context of water treatment, this process is used to break down contaminants in the water, making it safe for consumption or use. The water to be treated is passed through an electrolytic cell, where it is subjected to an electric current.

Electric purifier

A water purifier uses a multi-stage process involving UV and UF filtration, carbon block, and modern water filtration technology that eliminates most of the chemicals and impurities, making it the purest drinking water.

About the water treatment

Environmental water treatment is essential for cleaning and managing water sources, making them safe for humans and ecosystems. It removes contaminants like pathogens, chemicals, and heavy metals from water bodies affected by pollution from industrial, agricultural, and domestic sources.

Electrostatic water treatment system

An electrostatic water treatment system removes contaminants from water using an electric field rather than chemicals. Key components include: 1. Electrostatic Cell: Charges particles in the water to neutralize and remove them. 2. Cooling Tower: Lowers water temperature to reduce scaling. 3. Centrifugal Pump: Ensures consistent water flow. 4. Cross-Flow Mixer: Mixes water thoroughly, helping to distribute charges. 5. Filters: Capture remaining particles post-treatment. This system will be used in industrial settings to reduce the water pollution without any chemicals
